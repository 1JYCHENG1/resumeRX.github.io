An Online Travel Time Prediction Model Based on Functional Data Analysis

Renxin Zhong, Jiachen Luo, Hengxing Cai, Agachai Sumalee, Fangfang Yuan, Andy Hf Chow
Jan, 2017
URL

Abstract

Travel time is an important performance index for managers to evaluate the performance of transportation systems while a most intuitive measure for travelers to make route choices and departure time. An important part of the literature focuses on predicting instantaneous travel time under recurrent traffic conditions for traffic information dissemination. However, accurate travel time prediction is of importance for accessing the impacts and helping travelers make reliable travel decisions under abnormal traffic conditions. This study proposes an online travel time prediction model with emphasis on capturing the effects caused by anomalies. The model divides a path into short links. A Functional Principal Component Analysis (FPCA) framework is adopted to forecast link travel times based on historical data and real-time measurements. Further, a probabilistic nested delay operator is used to calculate path travel time distributions. To ensure the algorithm fast enough for online applications, a parallel computation architecture is introduced to overcome the computational burden of the FPCA. Finally, a rolling horizon structure is applied for on-line travel time prediction. The empirical results on Guangzhou Airport Expressway indicate that the proposed method can capture the abrupt change in traffic state and provide a promising and reliable travel time prediction at both link and path levels. In the case when original FPCA has been modified for parallelization, the accuracy and computational effort are evaluated in comparison with those of the sequential algorithm. It is found that the proposed algorithm requires a single rather than large set of traffic incident records.
